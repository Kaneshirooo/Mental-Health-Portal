INTRODUCTION
Project Context
Technology's rapid development had profoundly impacted numerous industries, including communication, business, and education. Individuals increasingly depended on internet-based systems—such as social networks, e-commerce, and e-learning platforms—because these provided personalized services based on users' past interactions. According to Riza Shafiloo et al. (2021), personalization enhanced the user experience by delivering tailored content and efficient services that responded to specific needs. Aligned with this technological shift, the Pangasinan State University–San Carlos Campus Web-Based Guidance System integrated automated record management, appointment scheduling, and employee reporting features to modernize the guidance office's manual and traditional procedures. By utilizing a data-driven and customized approach, the system improved accessibility, reduced errors, and ensured that instructors and students received relevant and timely support. The digital storage and retrieval of guidance-related documents also enhanced operational efficiency by promoting a more structured and responsive student assistance system.
According to D. Y. Sari and W. Yahyan (2024), students were required to meet with their supervisors in person as part of the Guidance Process, which was traditionally conducted face-to-face. However, several challenges hindered the effective implementation of this process. These included limited communication time, incompatible schedules between students and lecturers, difficulties in monitoring guidance progress due to large student loads, supervisors' activities or personal matters that caused delays, financial constraints faced by students, and the reliance on paper-based files that resulted in wasted resources during revisions.
Livamianti et al. (2024), emphasized that maintaining student discipline was a crucial step in creating a productive and comfortable learning environment. To ensure timely and protocol-based corrective measures, discipline violations needed close monitoring. However, many schools still recorded and handled disciplinary cases manually. This conventional method—typically involving written logs or physical archives—was time-consuming, prone to data loss, vulnerable to inaccuracies, and often resulted in delayed responses. Such issues made it increasingly difficult to properly manage and address student infractions.
To expand on this, R. Livamianti et al. (2024), explained that a technology-based solution was necessary to improve the effectiveness and accuracy of student disciplinary management. A web-based tool could digitize the documentation, tracking, and reporting of student disciplinary violations more efficiently. Through web-based technology, this system enabled real-time, accessible, and more accurate data management for administrators and students.
Pangasinan State University (PSU) San Carlos Campus, located at Roxas Blvd., San Carlos, Pangasinan 2420, was recognized as a distinguished institution committed to providing quality higher education and promoting academic excellence in the region. Offering programs such as Bachelor of Elementary Education, Bachelor of Secondary Education, Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, the campus had supported thousands of students through its dedicated employees and administrative staff. However, as enrollment increased, the campus faced growing challenges in managing student data, counseling assessments, and employee responsibilities. The institution relied on a manual information management system dependent on paperwork and scattered digital files. This outdated method resulted in inefficiencies such as data discrepancies, delayed processing, and difficulties in resource allocation, all of which hindered effective decision-making and academic support.
The Guidance Office of PSU San Carlos Campus continued to encode and maintain student data using antiquated manual procedures. These processes were time-consuming and highly prone to human error, increasing the likelihood of misplaced or lost student documentation. For example, students received printed inventory forms that they had to return to the office after completion. Administrators were then required to manually sort and organize these bulky paper documents, creating an arduous and time-intensive task.
According to R. G. Guntara et al. (2023), manual procedures were typically less efficient and effective than automated ones. The guidance office’s reliance on traditional methods significantly limited its operational capacity, causing unnecessary delays and reducing the overall quality of services provided to students. Furthermore, the possibility of misplacing important information during the paperwork management process heightened the risk of additional administrative problems.
Guntara et al. (2023), further emphasized that their research aimed to develop an innovative, effective, and standardized web-based evaluation and supervision model. This involved system design, testing, and integration to create a website-based evaluation and monitoring system. Using multi-agent technology and a client/server architecture, the system enabled users to efficiently access databases and relevant information to perform their tasks.
Yahya (2020), stated that arranging counseling sessions for students needing guidance remained a major concern. Without an efficient scheduling mechanism, students faced significant challenges when attempting to meet with guidance counselors. Schedule conflicts resulted in delays and missed counseling opportunities, leading to frustration among both students and administrators. The lack of clear communication about available appointment times further contributed to misunderstandings. Manual management also increased the risk of double bookings or overlooked appointments.
As the student population continued to grow, the administrative workload associated with maintaining records and counseling schedules intensified. The inefficiencies of the existing system overwhelmed guidance office personnel, potentially leading to burnout and reduced job satisfaction. These challenges also increased stress levels among administrators and students, who struggled to keep up with the demands of a manual system. As a result, there was a pressing need for a streamlined and efficient method of managing student data and counseling processes to enhance the office’s ability to provide timely support while reducing administrative burdens.
The proposed Web-Based Guidance System was envisioned to significantly improve the operations of the PSU San Carlos Campus Guidance Office by addressing long-standing issues associated with manual data management and inefficient service delivery. For students, the system offered a more accessible and user-friendly platform to schedule counseling sessions, receive timely support, and track their guidance records, fostering both academic and personal development. Employees benefited from streamlined reporting tools and real-time access to guidance data, allowing them to provide more informed and responsive mentorship. For administrators, the system automated routine tasks, reduced paperwork, and provided data analytics for improved decision-making and resource allocation. By integrating these components into a centralized digital platform, the system contributed to a more efficient, responsive, and student-centered guidance service aligned with the institution’s commitment to academic excellence and student welfare.

1.4 Purpose and Description
The purpose of the capstone project entitled "Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University-San Carlos Campus" was to develop a system that lessens and refines the manual process of managing. This project's main goal is to provide a more accurate, accessible, and efficient system that reduces administrative burdens and enhances the standard of services offered to staff, professors, and students. Ineffective record administration, schedule conflicts, human errors in documentation, and laborious manual transactions are only a few of the issues with the conventional paper-based system. The system aims to address these issues and implement a more automated and structured method of guidance-related operations by switching to a web-based platform.
The automation of studentinventory forms, which removes the need for students to manually complete paper-based forms, is a key component of this system. This guarantees that records are safely kept and may be quickly retrieved when needed, in addition to lowering data encoding errors. 
Faculty members can also report issues or send students to the guidance office more easily and effectively thanks to the system. Faculty can save time and effort while preserving the authenticity and confidentiality of their reports by submitting them online rather than in person at the office and enduring tiresome procedures.
Additionally, the use of this web-based platform brings with it an improved scheduling/appointment system that makes it easy for staff and students to make appointments with the guidance office. Ineffective time management and schedule conflicts are common outcomes of the conventional walk-in consultation approach. By using this technology, guidance counselors can better manage their calendars and students may make online appointment bookings, guaranteeing a seamless consultation process free from needless delays or conflicting appointments. In furtherance of enhancing scheduling and documentation, the system will act as a consolidated database for records pertaining to guidance, which will facilitate administrators' ability to keep an eye on student concerns, monitor progress, and produce reports as needed. The Guidance Office's decision-making procedures are improved by this data-driven approach, which also guarantees that all necessary data is accurately recorded and available when needed.
This initiative aimed to replace outdated manual scheduling methods with a robust, user-friendly online platform that facilitates better client interactions and improves operational efficiency. Overall, the Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus is expected to have a significant and transformative impact on the operational efficiency of the Guidance Office. By leveraging modern technology, it not only minimizes the administrative burden on guidance counselors and staff but also provides students and faculty with a more accessible, reliable, and user-friendly platform for communication and support. This initiative aligns with the university’s goal of integrating technology-driven solutions to improve institutional processes, ultimately contributing to a more efficient and student-centered academic environment.
1.5 Scope and Limitations
This study primarily focused on the development of the Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University–San Carlos Campus. In this section, the developers outlined the scope and limitations of the system as encountered during the development cycle. The system was designed with three levels of user access—Administrator, Employees, and Students—each configured to meet specific operational needs and assigned privileges within the platform.
The Administrator account, which oversaw the entire system, possessed the highest level of access and authority. It managed employee and student accounts, roles, permissions, student records, appointment scheduling, and announcements while ensuring the accuracy of the stored data. By restricting certain functions to prevent unauthorized modifications, the system maintained both security and platform stability. Administrators also had the capability to restore archived data when necessary and exclusive access to system logs for activity monitoring.
Students were able to easily review their guidance records through a personalized dashboard that allowed them to track any recorded concerns, previous consultations, or interventions made by the Guidance Office. They could also complete and submit their individual inventory forms online, removing the need for physical paperwork and minimizing potential data entry errors. Additionally, students could view the Administrator’s latest announcements, ensuring that they remained informed about important updates regarding policies, service schedules, or campus events.
Employees, on the other hand, were able to submit detailed reports regarding their concerns about students, providing the Guidance Office with a more structured and efficient method of communication. Through this feature, teachers could record academic, behavioral, or personal concerns, ensuring that these issues were documented accurately and addressed promptly. By filing reports online, employees eliminated the need to visit the Guidance Office in person, thereby streamlining the reporting process and supporting faster intervention when necessary. The system also allowed employees to monitor the status of their submitted reports, promoting transparency and strengthening collaboration between educators and guidance personnel in supporting students’ academic performance and overall well-being.
Despite its advantages, the system had several limitations. It was intended solely for use within the Guidance Office of Pangasinan State University–San Carlos Campus. Since the system depended on stable internet connectivity, any network interruptions could affect its accessibility and functionality. Furthermore, while the system enhanced digital record-keeping and online transactions, in-person counseling sessions remained essential for providing comprehensive guidance and support, as digital tools could not fully replace personal interaction. The system’s effectiveness also relied heavily on user compliance and active participation from administrators, instructors, and students.
Another important limitation was the exclusion of psychological test questions from the system. These assessment instruments could not be integrated into the platform because they were legally protected and restricted from public distribution. To comply with educational and ethical regulations, the system only accommodated general records and documentation, without including any proprietary or confidential psychological testing materials.
1.6 Definition of Terms
In this section, the developers provided clear and precise definitions of key terms and concepts integral to understanding the capstone project. This ensured consistency and clarity throughout the document, allowing readers to fully grasp the subject matter without ambiguity. The following terms were defined for reference:
Faculty: the entire teaching and administrative force of a university, college, or school.The teaching staff and academic staff at Pangasinan State University-San Carlos Campus who use the Web-Based Guidance System to report student concerns, monitor the status of reported cases, and interact with the Guidance Office about student behavior, academic performance, or well-being are referred to as faculty in this study.
Counseling Appointment:  Is a planned meeting that is set up via the Web-Based Guidance System between a student and a guidance counselor. Students can seek advice and help about academic, personal, or emotional issues during this visit, which guarantees a well-organized and planned consultation procedure to successfully meet their needs.
Cross-Device: The capacity of a system to work flawlessly across a variety of platforms, such as desktop computers, tablets, and smartphones, is referred to in this study as cross-device. This feature enhances convenience, accessibility, and system usability by guaranteeing that users—including administrators, instructors, and students—can access the Web-Based Guidance System at any time and from any location.
Functional Requirements: the specific functions and features that the Guidance System for Pangasinan State University – San Carlos Campus is designed to perform. These include core system operations such as user account registration and login, appointment scheduling between students and counselors, storing and retrieving counseling records, managing student profiles, and generating reports. Functional requirements define what the system should do to support the day-to-day processes of the Guidance Office, ensuring that guidance counselors, administrators, and students can efficiently perform their tasks within the digital platform.
Non-Functional Requirements:The specific quality attributes that the Guidance System for Pangasinan State University – San Carlos Campus must meet to ensure dependable and user-centered operation. These include system performance (such as fast response time during data access), data security through encrypted authentication methods like JSON Web Tokens (JWT), user-friendly interface design for ease of use by guidance counselors, students, and administrators, and system reliability through regular backups and fault tolerance. These requirements determine how well the system performs under various operational conditions and directly impact its usability, security, and stability.

Project Context
Technology's rapid development had profoundly impacted numerous industries, including communication, business, and education. Individuals increasingly depended on internet-based systems—such as social networks, e-commerce, and e-learning platforms—because these provided personalized services based on users' past interactions. According to Riza Shafiloo et al. (2021), personalization enhanced the user experience by delivering tailored content and efficient services that responded to specific needs. Aligned with this technological shift, the Pangasinan State University–San Carlos Campus Web-Based Guidance System integrated automated record management, appointment scheduling, and employee reporting features to modernize the guidance office's manual and traditional procedures. By utilizing a data-driven and customized approach, the system improved accessibility, reduced errors, and ensured that instructors and students received relevant and timely support. The digital storage and retrieval of guidance-related documents also enhanced operational efficiency by promoting a more structured and responsive student assistance system.
According to D. Y. Sari and W. Yahyan (2024), students were required to meet with their supervisors in person as part of the Guidance Process, which was traditionally conducted face-to-face. However, several challenges hindered the effective implementation of this process. These included limited communication time, incompatible schedules between students and lecturers, difficulties in monitoring guidance progress due to large student loads, supervisors' activities or personal matters that caused delays, financial constraints faced by students, and the reliance on paper-based files that resulted in wasted resources during revisions.
Livamianti et al. (2024), emphasized that maintaining student discipline was a crucial step in creating a productive and comfortable learning environment. To ensure timely and protocol-based corrective measures, discipline violations needed close monitoring. However, many schools still recorded and handled disciplinary cases manually. This conventional method—typically involving written logs or physical archives—was time-consuming, prone to data loss, vulnerable to inaccuracies, and often resulted in delayed responses. Such issues made it increasingly difficult to properly manage and address student infractions.
To expand on this, R. Livamianti et al. (2024), explained that a technology-based solution was necessary to improve the effectiveness and accuracy of student disciplinary management. A web-based tool could digitize the documentation, tracking, and reporting of student disciplinary violations more efficiently. Through web-based technology, this system enabled real-time, accessible, and more accurate data management for administrators and students.
Pangasinan State University (PSU) San Carlos Campus, located at Roxas Blvd., San Carlos, Pangasinan 2420, was recognized as a distinguished institution committed to providing quality higher education and promoting academic excellence in the region. Offering programs such as Bachelor of Elementary Education, Bachelor of Secondary Education, Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, the campus had supported thousands of students through its dedicated employees and administrative staff. However, as enrollment increased, the campus faced growing challenges in managing student data, counseling assessments, and employee responsibilities. The institution relied on a manual information management system dependent on paperwork and scattered digital files. This outdated method resulted in inefficiencies such as data discrepancies, delayed processing, and difficulties in resource allocation, all of which hindered effective decision-making and academic support.
The Guidance Office of PSU San Carlos Campus continued to encode and maintain student data using antiquated manual procedures. These processes were time-consuming and highly prone to human error, increasing the likelihood of misplaced or lost student documentation. For example, students received printed inventory forms that they had to return to the office after completion. Administrators were then required to manually sort and organize these bulky paper documents, creating an arduous and time-intensive task.
According to R. G. Guntara et al. (2023), manual procedures were typically less efficient and effective than automated ones. The guidance office’s reliance on traditional methods significantly limited its operational capacity, causing unnecessary delays and reducing the overall quality of services provided to students. Furthermore, the possibility of misplacing important information during the paperwork management process heightened the risk of additional administrative problems.
Guntara et al. (2023), further emphasized that their research aimed to develop an innovative, effective, and standardized web-based evaluation and supervision model. This involved system design, testing, and integration to create a website-based evaluation and monitoring system. Using multi-agent technology and a client/server architecture, the system enabled users to efficiently access databases and relevant information to perform their tasks.
Yahya (2020), stated that arranging counseling sessions for students needing guidance remained a major concern. Without an efficient scheduling mechanism, students faced significant challenges when attempting to meet with guidance counselors. Schedule conflicts resulted in delays and missed counseling opportunities, leading to frustration among both students and administrators. The lack of clear communication about available appointment times further contributed to misunderstandings. Manual management also increased the risk of double bookings or overlooked appointments.
As the student population continued to grow, the administrative workload associated with maintaining records and counseling schedules intensified. The inefficiencies of the existing system overwhelmed guidance office personnel, potentially leading to burnout and reduced job satisfaction. These challenges also increased stress levels among administrators and students, who struggled to keep up with the demands of a manual system. As a result, there was a pressing need for a streamlined and efficient method of managing student data and counseling processes to enhance the office’s ability to provide timely support while reducing administrative burdens.
The proposed Web-Based Guidance System was envisioned to significantly improve the operations of the PSU San Carlos Campus Guidance Office by addressing long-standing issues associated with manual data management and inefficient service delivery. For students, the system offered a more accessible and user-friendly platform to schedule counseling sessions, receive timely support, and track their guidance records, fostering both academic and personal development. Employees benefited from streamlined reporting tools and real-time access to guidance data, allowing them to provide more informed and responsive mentorship. For administrators, the system automated routine tasks, reduced paperwork, and provided data analytics for improved decision-making and resource allocation. By integrating these components into a centralized digital platform, the system contributed to a more efficient, responsive, and student-centered guidance service aligned with the institution’s commitment to academic excellence and student welfare.

1.5 Scope and Limitations
This study primarily focused on the development of the Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University–San Carlos Campus. In this section, the developers outlined the scope and limitations of the system as encountered during the development cycle. The system was designed with three levels of user access—Administrator, Employees, and Students—each configured to meet specific operational needs and assigned privileges within the platform.
The Administrator account, which oversaw the entire system, possessed the highest level of access and authority. It managed employee and student accounts, roles, permissions, student records, appointment scheduling, and announcements while ensuring the accuracy of the stored data. By restricting certain functions to prevent unauthorized modifications, the system maintained both security and platform stability. Administrators also had the capability to restore archived data when necessary and exclusive access to system logs for activity monitoring.
Students were able to easily review their guidance records through a personalized dashboard that allowed them to track any recorded concerns, previous consultations, or interventions made by the Guidance Office. They could also complete and submit their individual inventory forms online, removing the need for physical paperwork and minimizing potential data entry errors. Additionally, students could view the Administrator’s latest announcements, ensuring that they remained informed about important updates regarding policies, service schedules, or campus events.
Employees, on the other hand, were able to submit detailed reports regarding their concerns about students, providing the Guidance Office with a more structured and efficient method of communication. Through this feature, teachers could record academic, behavioral, or personal concerns, ensuring that these issues were documented accurately and addressed promptly. By filing reports online, employees eliminated the need to visit the Guidance Office in person, thereby streamlining the reporting process and supporting faster intervention when necessary. The system also allowed employees to monitor the status of their submitted reports, promoting transparency and strengthening collaboration between educators and guidance personnel in supporting students’ academic performance and overall well-being.
Despite its advantages, the system had several limitations. It was intended solely for use within the Guidance Office of Pangasinan State University–San Carlos Campus. Since the system depended on stable internet connectivity, any network interruptions could affect its accessibility and functionality. Furthermore, while the system enhanced digital record-keeping and online transactions, in-person counseling sessions remained essential for providing comprehensive guidance and support, as digital tools could not fully replace personal interaction. The system’s effectiveness also relied heavily on user compliance and active participation from administrators, instructors, and students.
Another important limitation was the exclusion of psychological test questions from the system. These assessment instruments could not be integrated into the platform because they were legally protected and restricted from public distribution. To comply with educational and ethical regulations, the system only accommodated general records and documentation, without including any proprietary or confidential psychological testing materials.

2.1 Technical Background
In order to guarantee dependability, scalability, and usability, the technologies, frameworks, and design principles utilized in the development of the Web-Based Guidance System for PSUSC are described in detail in this section of the document.
JavaScript was used as the primary programming language for both the front-end and back-end development, providing consistency across the entire development stack. This uniform approach reduced the learning curve for developers, simplified debugging, and accelerated the creation of system features. The back-end operated on Node.js, a runtime environment designed for real-time and event-driven applications. Node.js efficiently handled asynchronous tasks and multiple concurrent requests, making it suitable for managing numerous appointment bookings without performance issues. Express.js, a lightweight framework for Node.js, further supported server-side development by offering organized routing, middleware integration, and structured API management.
For data management, MongoDB was used as the system’s database. As a NoSQL database, MongoDB accommodated flexible, evolving, and unstructured data—an important requirement for handling user profiles, appointment records, and system logs that changed over time. Its schema-less design and ability to scale horizontally ensured fast data retrieval and stable performance as system usage increased. This allowed the system to adapt to growth without the need for major database restructuring.
RESTful APIs were implemented to manage communication between system components. These APIs followed standard HTTP protocols, making data exchange lightweight, secure, and platform-independent. Through this architecture, the system maintained smooth interactions between the client interface, server processes, and database operations. The use of RESTful APIs also supported potential integration with external services and future system enhancements.
Security was addressed through the implementation of JSON Web Tokens (JWT) for authentication and session handling. JWT provided a secure, stateless method of verifying user identities, reducing risks associated with session hijacking and unauthorized access. Its stateless nature improved scalability by eliminating the need for storing session data on the server.
The system was deployed on Vercel, a cloud hosting platform known for its reliability and automated deployment capabilities. Vercel provided continuous integration, serverless functions, and automatic scaling, ensuring that updates were deployed without interrupting system availability. This environment maintained stable system performance even during periods of high user activity.
Throughout development, Visual Studio Code functioned as the primary code editor, supplying debugging tools, syntax support, and productivity-enhancing extensions. GitHub was utilized for version control, allowing developers to track code changes, manage branches, and collaborate efficiently. Postman was used to test and validate API endpoints, enabling the team to detect and resolve issues early in the development process. By simulating real user scenarios, Postman ensured reliable API behavior and stable system performance.
Together, these technologies formed a robust and scalable system architecture that supported the needs of the Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus Guidance Office. The selected development tools, frameworks, and deployment platform enabled efficient operation, secure data handling, and a reliable appointment scheduling experience for its users.

4.2 Technical Solutions
Technical solutions referred to the strategies, methods, and processes implemented to address the functional and non-functional requirements of the system. These solutions defined how technologies were applied to solve problems, optimize performance, and meet user needs effectively. In developing Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus, the technical solutions were designed to ensure scalability, security, and usability while maintaining system efficiency and delivering a seamless user experience.  
The system architecture employed a client-server model, which separated the front-end user interface from the back-end infrastructure. This architecture ensured that the user-facing application remained lightweight and responsive while delegating complex processing tasks to the server. By isolating responsibilities, the system became easier to manage, maintain, and scale as user demand grew. This design supported future enhancements without requiring significant changes to the core infrastructure, making it a robust choice for long-term use.  
JavaScript was selected as the programming language for both the front-end and back-end development, ensuring consistency across the development stack. This uniformity reduced the learning curve for developers, streamlined debugging, and accelerated feature development. The back-end leveraged Node.js, a runtime environment well-suited for real-time, event-driven applications. Node.js efficiently handled asynchronous operations and concurrent connections, which were critical for managing multiple appointment bookings without performance bottlenecks. By using JavaScript and Node.js, the system remained agile, adaptable, and capable of supporting real-time user interactions.  
To handle data storage and retrieval, MongoDB was chosen as the database solution. Unlike traditional relational databases, MongoDB, as a NoSQL database, offered flexibility in managing dynamic and unstructured data. This capability was particularly important for the appointment booking system, where data structures evolved as new features were added. MongoDB’s ability to scale horizontally ensured that as the number of users and appointments increased, system performance remained unaffected. Additionally, its fast query performance reduced latency, providing a smoother experience for users accessing and managing their appointments.  
The integration of RESTful APIs facilitated standardized and efficient communication between system components. RESTful APIs adhered to HTTP protocols, making them lightweight and platform-independent. This ensured secure and reliable data exchange between the front-end, back-end, and database, enabling smooth system functionality. Furthermore, the RESTful architecture allowed for seamless integration with third-party tools or future system enhancements, ensuring the system remained adaptable to changing business requirements.  
Security was a key consideration for the system, which managed sensitive user data. To address this, JSON Web Tokens (JWT) were implemented for user authentication. JWT provided a secure mechanism for managing user sessions and ensuring that only authorized users could access the system. By using tokens for authentication, the system reduced vulnerabilities to common security threats such as session hijacking. JWT's stateless nature also improved system performance by eliminating the need to store session data on the server, which was particularly useful for scaling the application.  
The system was deployed on Vercel, a cloud hosting platform known for its reliability, scalability, and developer-friendly features. Vercel provided automated deployment, continuous integration, and serverless capabilities, which ensured that updates could be released quickly without disrupting system operations. Its ability to scale infrastructure based on user demand guaranteed consistent performance even during peak usage periods. Vercel’s robust infrastructure also enhanced system uptime, ensuring that the appointment booking system remained accessible and reliable at all times.  
Testing played a crucial role in ensuring the stability and functionality of the system. Postman was used to rigorously test API endpoints and interactions between system components. By simulating real-world usage scenarios, Postman helped identify issues early in the development process, reducing the risk of errors in production. This testing approach ensured that the system delivered consistent and reliable performance, even under varying workloads.  
Version control and collaboration were managed using GitHub, a widely adopted platform that enabled developers to track changes, manage code versions, and collaborate effectively. GitHub’s branching and merging capabilities ensured that updates and new features could be developed, tested, and integrated without disrupting the core system. This approach enhanced code quality, reduced errors, and allowed multiple developers to work on the project simultaneously, ensuring a streamlined and efficient development process.  
4.3 Technology Used
Technology played an essential role in the development of the Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus. Selecting the right tools, programming languages, and platforms was crucial in ensuring that the system became reliable, scalable, and capable of meeting user needs. According to Almarabeh (2020), the appropriate choice of technology greatly influences a system’s performance, usability, and ability to support institutional processes effectively. In this project, the technologies used served as the core foundation for building features such as appointment scheduling, form requests, announcements, and referrals, ensuring that the system operates efficiently and provides a smooth experience for students, employees, and administrators.
The system was developed using JavaScript as the core programming language, with Express.js as the back-end framework. JavaScript is a versatile and widely adopted programming language that facilitated development consistency between the front-end and back-end. Express.js, a lightweight Node.js framework, simplified server-side development by providing efficient routing, middleware integration, and organized API management. This combination allowed the system to process requests quickly, ensuring smooth operations and faster response times.

4.7 Evaluation of the Proposed Computer-Based Solution
This section evaluates the Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus with emphasis on its acceptability using the ISO/IEC 25010 Software Quality Model. Acceptability refers to the degree to which users consider the system adequate, appropriate, and satisfactory for accomplishing their tasks. Under ISO 25010, acceptability is influenced by software characteristics such as functional suitability, reliability, security, performance efficiency, compatibility, and maintainability, which together determine whether users are willing to adopt and rely on the system.
ISO 25010 was selected for this study because recent research has demonstrated its effectiveness in assessing user acceptance and overall system quality in educational and administrative environments (Januhari et al., 2025; Sarwosri et al., 2023). The standard’s focus on functional completeness, performance, and reliability makes it a suitable framework for evaluating systems that handle documentation, appointments, announcements, and referrals—core services offered through the Guidance System.
The results of the evaluation show that the Guidance System achieved a high level of acceptability among its users. Respondents agreed that the system’s functions—such as requesting certificates (e.g., good moral and psychological test certificates), scheduling appointments, viewing announcements, and processing student referrals—are relevant and helpful in supporting campus guidance services. Reliability contributed strongly to acceptability, as users experienced consistent performance with minimal system errors. Likewise, security measures enhanced user trust by ensuring that sensitive student data and referral information were kept confidential. The system was also evaluated as acceptable in terms of performance and compatibility, running efficiently across commonly used devices within the university environment.

SUMMARY, CONCLUSION, AND RECOMMENDATIONS
This chapter presents the summary, conclusions, and recommendations of the study entitled “Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus.” It revisits the project’s objectives, the methods used, and the results derived from the system evaluation. It highlights how the developed system addressed existing challenges in the university’s guidance processes—particularly appointment booking, referral handling, announcements dissemination, and certificate request processing. It also outlines recommendations aimed at further improving system performance, usability, and sustainability.
5.1 Summary
The study aimed to design and develop a Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus in response to the limitations of the existing manual and paper-based guidance office processes. These traditional procedures, which relied heavily on physical records and manual encoding, resulted in delays, inefficiencies, data redundancy, and difficulty in retrieving student information. Using a developmental-descriptive research approach and the Agile development framework, the researchers developed a web-based system that centralizes student records, inventory form submission, faculty reporting, and appointment scheduling. The system provides separate portals for administrators, faculty members, and students, allowing streamlined access to guidance services and efficient management of guidance office operations.
System acceptability was evaluated using a questionnaire based on the ISO/IEC 25010 software quality model and administered to respondents composed of guidance office administrators, faculty members, and students of Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus. The results of the system evaluation, as presented in Table 4.11, showed that the system achieved an overall Average Weighted Mean (AWM) of 4.01, verbally interpreted as “Acceptable.” Dimension-wise, the system obtained a weighted mean of 4.10 for Functional Suitability, 3.99 for Performance Efficiency, 4.02 for Compatibility, 3.98 for Usability, 3.97 for Reliability, 4.01 for Security, 3.96 for Maintainability, and 4.04 for Portability, all of which were interpreted as Acceptable. These findings indicate that the system met the required software quality standards and satisfied the expectations of its intended users.
Overall, the study successfully produced a web-based guidance system that streamlines student record management, inventory submission, faculty reporting, and appointment scheduling for the Guidance Office of Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus. The system effectively addressed the inefficiencies of the previous manual process by providing a centralized, secure, and user-friendly platform for guidance services. The favorable overall rating of 4.01 (Acceptable) confirms that the intended users generally find the system efficient, reliable, and useful, while also highlighting opportunities for future enhancements to further strengthen system reliability, scalability, and long-term sustainability.
Conclusions
The Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus successfully addressed the challenges of managing guidance services through a manual, paper-based process. The system was developed in response to the increasing demand for efficient student record management, appointment scheduling, and faculty reporting within the Guidance Office. The following conclusions were drawn based on the objectives of the project:
1.The study successfully designed a streamlined digital information management and guidance process for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus Guidance Office. In the developed system, student inventory forms are submitted online, faculty concerns are reported through a web-based platform, and counseling appointments are scheduled digitally, with all records automatically stored in a centralized database. This digital workflow replaced the traditional paper-based documentation and manual encoding procedures, reducing processing delays, minimizing human error, and preventing data loss. The new process enables the Guidance Office to efficiently retrieve student records, monitor counseling activities, track reported concerns, generate reports, and provide timely guidance interventions.
2. The system was designed to meet both the functional and non-functional requirements identified during the analysis phase. Functionally, it enables administrators to manage student records, inventory forms, counseling appointments, faculty reports, and announcements, while allowing students to access their guidance records and schedule appointments online. Faculty members are also able to submit student concerns electronically and monitor the status of their reports. Non-functionally, the system was developed with considerations for performance efficiency, security, usability, reliability, compatibility, maintainability, and accessibility across devices. These requirements guided the design and implementation of a system that operates efficiently, protects sensitive student information, supports future enhancements, and ensures dependable service delivery for the Guidance Office. Furthermore, these same requirements served as the basis for testing and evaluating the system, ensuring that both its features and quality attributes performed as intended.
3.The system demonstrated a favorable reception among administrators, faculty members, and students based on the ISO/IEC 25010 software quality model. The overall evaluation results indicated that users found the system functionally suitable, efficient in performance, user-friendly, compatible with their workflows, secure, maintainable, and reliable. The overall average weighted mean of 4.01 likewise fell under the “Acceptable” range, demonstrating that users generally agreed that the system performs well in terms of functionality, efficiency, and overall quality. The respondents expressed positive feedback regarding the system’s ease of use, accessibility, and effectiveness in improving guidance office operations. The findings confirm that the system meets institutional requirements and user expectations and is ready for implementation within the Guidance Office of Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus.
The Web-Based Guidance System for Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus is a highly effective, reliable, and scalable solution that streamlines digital record management, online appointment scheduling, faculty reporting, and centralized monitoring of student concerns. It significantly improves the efficiency, accuracy, and responsiveness of guidance services while supporting the academic and personal development of students. Overall, the development of the system successfully met its intended objectives and contributed a practical and innovative solution to modernizing the guidance management process of Pangasinan State University San Carlos Campus.
